# Due Diligence

## Purpose

Due diligence is the lightweight structured evaluation used for stage
promotion and graduation decisions.

## Scope

Use it when a reviewer needs a clearer decision record than a checklist
alone provides.

It should stay small and practical.

## Inputs

- the relevant stage checklist
- application evidence
- release history
- contributor activity
- project documentation
- known risks or gaps

## Validation

Check whether the evidence is real, current, and sufficient for the
target stage.

Use binary questions where possible:

- is the license present
- are tests available
- is the project documented
- is there a maintenance path
- is there visible ownership

## Qualitative signals

Record short notes on:

- project stability
- clarity of ownership
- contributor responsiveness
- adoption signals
- operational maturity

## Risk assessment

Record any risk that could slow or block the next stage.

Keep the list focused on practical issues such as:

- missing maintainers
- unclear release process
- weak documentation
- untested critical paths
- security or deployment ambiguity

## Output

The due-diligence result should end with one of these statements:

- ready to promote
- ready with follow-up items
- not ready yet
