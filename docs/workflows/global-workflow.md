# Global Workflow

## Purpose

This is the canonical workflow for applications, review, iteration, and
promotion.

## Scope

Use this flow for all stage requests and promotion decisions.

## Sequence

Apply -> Review -> Feedback -> Accept -> Iterate -> Promote -> Evaluate

## Rules

- Async first: reviewers and authors do not need live meetings to move
  work forward.
- Checklist-driven: decisions must point back to the relevant criteria.
- Mentor-guided: mentors help authors close gaps, but they do not own
  approval.
- Editor-led transitions: editors approve stage movement and maintain
  consistency.
- Evidence-based: issues, releases, docs, and test results are better
  than narrative claims.

## Role behavior

### Authors

- submit the application
- provide evidence
- respond to feedback
- update the project until the checklist is satisfied

### Reviewers

- validate the evidence against the checklist
- give specific follow-up items
- avoid subjective blocking
- keep feedback short and actionable

### Editors

- assign or confirm reviewers
- maintain the criteria and templates
- approve or reject stage transitions
- resolve inconsistencies across docs

## Output

The workflow ends in one of two states:

- accepted for the target stage
- returned for iteration with explicit follow-up items
